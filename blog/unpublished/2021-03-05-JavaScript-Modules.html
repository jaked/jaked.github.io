JavaScript Modules, what I wish I'd known

2021-03-05

Once someone asked me what I do, and when I told them I work as a computer programmer, they said something like "I bet that involves a lot of details". This person was right—programming involves a lot of details! Sometimes it's easy to feel buried in details, and the thought of needing to acquire a new set of details (say a new API, language, or platform) might call to mind the Myth of Sisyphus. But "one must imagine [programmers] happy"—maybe we can view the details as a colorful landscape to travel rather than a mountain to summit.

Still, we're trying to get somewhere eventually. A thing I find frustrating about learning a new system is that at first I can't see the overall map of the landscape; so I can't understand a lot of the details, or decide whether they're important to me, because I can't put them in context. After a period of struggle I start to build a mental map, and then learning comes much faster.
